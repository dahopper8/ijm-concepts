What we offer

Not a logo on a website.
A verifiable human rights program.

IJM doesn't provide certifications. We provide on-the-ground presence, remediation support, and independently audited outcome reports — the kind of evidence that withstands regulatory scrutiny and investor due diligence.

01
Supply chain assessment
IJM's in-country teams conduct direct assessment of your supply chain partners in high-risk regions — identifying forced labor exposure, documentation gaps, and remediation priorities. Not a desk audit. An on-the-ground review.
02
Active remediation support
Where exposure is identified, IJM works alongside your suppliers and local authorities to address root causes — training, legal support, worker protection protocols. This is what separates a remediation program from a monitoring program.
03
Verified annual reporting
An independently audited annual report covering assessment findings, remediation progress, and verified outcome metrics — produced to a standard your General Counsel, CSO, and investors can rely on and regulators can't challenge.
04
Regulatory response support
When a regulator, investor, or journalist asks about your supply chain human rights practices, IJM's documentation and methodology provides the credible, defensible answer. We have testified before regulatory bodies on behalf of partner companies.
05
Employee engagement program
IJM provides a structured employee education and engagement program — giving your workforce a meaningful connection to the supply chain justice work happening on their behalf. High retention and purpose correlation in partner companies.
06
Co-branded impact publication
An annual co-branded impact report — featuring your company's specific supply chain program, verified outcomes, and IJM's independent methodology statement. Designed for investor relations, ESG disclosure, and external stakeholder communication.

Major seafood importer — Thailand fishing supply chain
IJM embedded alongside a major US seafood importer's Thai supplier network following 2017 research showing 38% of Thai fishing vessel workers identified as trafficking victims. Three-year remediation program including worker documentation, employer training, and legal support for identified victims.
