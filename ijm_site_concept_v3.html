Six things that have to happen for someone to actually be free.

Not rescued. Not temporarily safe. Actually, permanently, legally free — with a rebuilt life and a system that won't let it happen again.

01

Someone has to find them

Investigators and trained officers identify victims in places that look like ordinary businesses, farms, and homes. The search doesn't stop until the person is located.

02

Someone has to get them out

Coordinated rescue operations — built around survivor safety, not spectacle — extract victims from trafficking rings, forced labor, and violent households.

03

Someone has to hold the perpetrator accountable

Lawyers build prosecutable cases. Investigators gather evidence that survives cross-examination. Trials happen. Sentences stick. Traffickers learn there are consequences.

04

Someone has to stay with them

Aftercare isn't a phase — it's a long commitment. Social workers, counselors, and economic recovery programs stay engaged until survivors are genuinely self-sufficient.

05

The system has to be fixed

Police training. Legislative reform. Prosecution protocols. The work doesn't scale until the local justice system can sustain it without outside support.

06

The outcome has to be proven

Third-party researchers measure community safety before and after. Because donors deserve proof, and survivors deserve a record that what happened to them wasn't forgotten.
