IJM's model is designed to work itself out of a program. When a justice system can protect its own people without outside support, IJM closes the chapter. Hover over any block. Filter by status. The strip tells the whole story at a glance.

The problem this solves: Most donors don't know IJM has closed programs. Child trafficking in Kolkata is down 86%. Trafficking in Cebu dropped 79%. Nine of nine JSS programs reduced violence 50–85%. This makes that proof record visible — without an ask attached.

What "closed" means
3
Independent criteria before IJM closes a chapter: sustained conviction rates, trained local prosecutors, and third-party verified reduction in violence. All three, not one or two.
Average program duration
15 yrs
Justice system transformation takes time. IJM commits to the full arc from first intervention to systemic reform to verified closure, not an early exit.
The record no one else holds
9/9
Nine out of nine Justice System Strengthening Projects in the last decade reduced slavery and violence by 50 to 85%. No other human rights organization publishes audited outcome data at this standard.
